Who will be there

The Circle

Sir John Ward & Lady Elisabeth Ward

To explain the story of Ward family it is necessary to go back a little. It is necessary indeed to go back to the middle of the nineteenth century, when an industrious and intelligent man called Sibert Ward, who had been head gardener at a grand place in Sussex and had married the cook, bought with his savings and hers a few acres north of London and set up as a market gardener.

.
.

Father Brown

Due to circumstance, the favourite hero from Chesterton’s novels happens to be present this evening in villa Rosemary. An unassuming innocent looking clergyman, who’s greatest strength, both as a priest and as a detective of crime, is his love and understanding of other people. He’s not there to judge, but to save souls.

Mr. J.J. Bell

A wealthy Scottish businessman who has ended up living an adventurous life in China, where he is a member of the Executive Council. An old friend of Sir Ward who has invited his fiancée Ms Esabel Elsom.

.
.

Chester, Duke of Brecon (Wales)

He comes from an old wealthy family and are married to his cousin, Dorothy, the duchess of Brecon. A conservative politician and member of House of Lords. He has recently announced his candidature for the prime minister post.

Dorothy, Duchess of Brecon (Wales)

Married to her cousin Chester, the duke of Brecon. Of course supportive of her husband’s political career, but her true passion is for gardening, and she is an award-winning grower of English roses.

.
.

Mr. Alexandre Bunin

Alexandre Bunin is the son of a third generation Russian capitalists, but has chosen to follow his dream and become an artist. He met the Wards in Beaulieu since they are interested in the arts. He is married to Mrs Ekaterina Shaforson, “his muse” and he supports the arts and artist in the community.

Mrs. Ekaterina Shafarson-Bunin

Mrs Ekaterina Bunin is the muse and wife of Mr Alexandre Bunin. She is somewhat shy in the aristocratic environments offered by the Wards.

.
.

Captain Alistair Campbell

Captain Campbell is the nephew of Sir Ward, hence a true gentleman. He is a military attaché in the British army, previously based in Istanbul and a veteran from World War One, the Balkans. Captain Campbell is unmarried.

Miss Florence Claudel

Miss Claudel is a former international model, now a well-known profile amongst artists in the Paris where she arranges intellectual salons and events. She don’t know the Wards personally and has been invited by her old friend Mr Karim Khan.

.
.

Ms. Esabel Elsom

Ms. Esabel is an aristocratic well-educated lady who studied at Howard College in England but is now a famous Hollywood actress. She is engaged with J.J. Bell, an old friend of Sir Ward.

Mr. Carl August Ericsson

Mr Ericsson is a Swedish arms dealer, entrepreneur - and a hobby photographer. An old friend of Sir Ward, he is now living in The New World, also known as America, and married to the eccentric Mrs Chesterton.

.
.

Mrs. Meredith Chesterton-Ericsson

Mrs Chesterton is a true American socialite. Rumor has it she is an apprentice to the famous spiritist Madame Blavatsky, and that she holds séances where she contacts dead relatives. Mrs Chesterton is married to Mr Ericsson. Her husband is a close friend to Lord Ward.

Mr. Giuseppe Ferrara

Giuseppe Ferrara is an Italian biomedical scientist who recently came up with a possible vaccine against the terrible Spanish flu. He developed the recipe for a decade together with his late wife dr Antonella Ferrara but the Spanish flue took her life. Invited by Carl August Ericson, he doesn’t know the Wards but his achievements make him an interesting character.

.
.

Mr David Wark Gruffith

An American film producer, a self-made man who has climbed his way from a poor childhood to the very top. Invited to the party by the young Ms Ward, the daughter in the house, who is rumoured to dream of becoming an actress.

Mr. Karim Khan

A successful international banker with origins from Persia, unmarried and living a roaming life, moving from country to country. Met Sir Ward before the war through his nephew Captain Alistair Campbell who was a British attaché at the time. Have invited his old friend Madame Florence Claudel, the Parisienne.

.
.

Ms. Eugenie Lescuyer

Ms Lescuyer is a professional pilot, one of the first pioneers of ladies-pilots. She is a widow, since less than one year and has travelled the world all by herself. It is said that she wanted to fight in the war but was dismissed because of her sex. She was invited by Lady Ward since she is a remarkable character.

Mr. Glenn Lucee

A former American diplomat who met the Wards in London. Unmarried, with family roots in Shanghai, and an urbane, charming man, the type of gentleman who seems to be immune to aging.

.
.

Mr. Donald McKenzie

Mr. Mc Kenzie is a successful Australian real estate mogul. A widower and an old friend of Sir Ward.

Princess Menshikova

A naïve sweet royal Russian, who also arrived recently in France. Unlike Countess Vronskaya, she hasn’t spent much time outside Russia before the revolution so no one really knows her, but her family name is at least a guarantor for nobility.

.
.

Mr. Jean Paul Mercadier

The wealthy French-American trader of champagne, famous for a marketing publicity stunt, for selling to the finest houses in Russia, before the revolution. He is married to the illustrious Cristel Sparre-Mercadier, and an old friend of Sir Ward.

Mrs. Cristel Sparre-Mercadier

Mrs Sparre is a writer and journalist with a flair for adventure. Originally from Sweden, and married to Mr Mercadier, the wealthy champagne producer.

.
.

Mr. Somerset Novartis

Mr Novartis is a successful entrepreneur in the expanding business of medicine. He and his wife are old friends of the Wards.

Mrs. Lydia Novartis

Mrs Lydia Novartis is the loyal wife to Mr Novartis. She appreciates classical music, fine arts and beautiful hats.

.
.

Mr Charles Rolls

Aviator, inventor and car manufacturer of noble background, the third son of Baron and Lady Llangattock in Wales. He knows the Ward family through their nephew Captain Alistair Campbell whom he partied in London with before the war.

Countess Anastasia Vronskaya

Crème de la crème of Russian aristocracy, not unfamiliar with life in Monaco and Baden Baden but always a faithful Russian, due to the unfortunate events in her home country she had to escape hastily and resettle here on the Cote D’Azur, were she met and became friends with Lady Ward.
